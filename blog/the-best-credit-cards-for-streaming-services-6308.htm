The Best Credit Cards for Streaming Services

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Streaming services have become an essential part of modern life, offering entertainment, news, and even fitness content at our fingertips. With the rise of remote work, global inflation, and the increasing cost of subscriptions, consumers are looking for ways to maximize their spending. One smart strategy? Using the right credit card to earn rewards, cash back, or even free subscriptions.

In this guide, we’ll break down the best credit cards for streaming services, helping you save money while enjoying your favorite shows, movies, and music.

Why Use a Credit Card for Streaming?

Before diving into the best cards, let’s discuss why using a credit card for streaming is a smart move:

1. Cash Back & Rewards

Many credit cards offer bonus rewards for streaming purchases, allowing you to earn points or cash back on subscriptions like Netflix, Spotify, or Disney+.

2. Subscription Credits

Some premium cards provide annual statement credits specifically for streaming services, effectively lowering your costs.

3. Fraud Protection & Convenience

Credit cards offer better fraud protection than debit cards, and automatic payments ensure you never miss an episode due to a lapsed subscription.

4. Global Accessibility

With geo-restrictions and currency fluctuations, certain cards (especially those with no foreign transaction fees) make it easier to access international streaming platforms.

Top Credit Cards for Streaming Services

Here are the best credit cards in 2024 for streaming enthusiasts:

1. Blue Cash Preferred® Card from American Express

Best for: High cash back on streaming

  • 6% cash back on select U.S. streaming subscriptions (Netflix, Hulu, HBO Max, etc.)
  • 6% cash back at U.S. supermarkets (up to $6,000 per year, then 1%)
  • 3% cash back on transit and gas
  • Annual fee: $95 (waived first year in some offers)

Why it’s great: If you spend heavily on both groceries and streaming, this card pays for itself quickly.

2. Capital One SavorOne Cash Rewards Credit Card

Best for: No annual fee + entertainment rewards

  • 3% cash back on streaming, dining, and entertainment
  • 10% cash back on Uber & Uber Eats (until Nov. 2024)
  • No annual fee
  • No foreign transaction fees

Why it’s great: A budget-friendly option with solid rewards for streaming and dining.

3. Chase Sapphire Preferred® Card

Best for: Travelers who stream

  • 3X points on streaming services (when booked through Chase Ultimate Rewards)
  • 5X points on travel booked through Chase
  • Annual fee: $95
  • Strong travel perks (transfer partners, rental car insurance)

Why it’s great: If you love both streaming and traveling, this card maximizes your rewards.

4. U.S. Bank Altitude® Go Visa Signature® Card

Best for: Foodies who stream

  • 4X points on dining (including food delivery apps)
  • 2X points on streaming services
  • No annual fee
  • $15 annual streaming credit after 11 months of streaming purchases

Why it’s great: The annual credit makes it a hidden gem for casual streamers.

5. The Platinum Card® from American Express

Best for: Luxury perks + streaming credits

  • Up to $240 in annual streaming credits ($20/month for Disney+, Hulu, ESPN+, Peacock, etc.)
  • 5X points on flights and hotels (booked through Amex Travel)
  • Annual fee: $695
  • Exclusive lounge access & concierge service

Why it’s great: If you’re already paying for premium streaming, this card’s credits can offset its high annual fee.

How to Maximize Your Streaming Rewards

1. Stack Subscriptions with Family Plans

Many streaming services (Spotify, YouTube Premium) offer family plans at discounted rates. Use a rewards card to pay, then split costs with friends or family.

2. Leverage Sign-Up Bonuses

Some cards offer big sign-up bonuses (e.g., 60,000 points after spending $4,000 in 3 months). Use these to cover subscription costs.

3. Combine with Cashback Apps

Apps like Rakuten sometimes offer extra cash back for streaming subscriptions when you shop through their portal.

4. Monitor Limited-Time Offers

Banks occasionally run promotions (e.g., "Spend $50 on streaming, get $10 back"). Always check your card’s offers section.

The Future of Streaming & Credit Card Rewards

As streaming becomes more fragmented (with services like Max, Apple TV+, and Paramount+ competing for attention), credit card companies are adapting. Expect to see:

  • More niche rewards (e.g., bonus points for gaming subscriptions like Xbox Game Pass)
  • Bundled perks (e.g., free months of Apple Music with certain cards)
  • Dynamic rewards (cards adjusting bonus categories based on your spending habits)

The key takeaway? Choose a card that aligns with your streaming habits—whether you’re a casual viewer or a binge-watching enthusiast.
