Best 0% Interest Credit Cards for Travel Rewards

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Traveling the world is a dream for many, but rising costs and economic uncertainties can make it challenging. Fortunately, 0% interest credit cards for travel rewards offer a smart way to explore without worrying about high-interest debt. Whether you're planning a solo adventure, a family vacation, or a business trip, these cards can help you save money while earning valuable perks.

Why Choose a 0% Interest Travel Rewards Card?

Financial Flexibility in Uncertain Times

With inflation affecting everything from airfare to hotel stays, managing expenses is crucial. A 0% introductory APR (Annual Percentage Rate) card allows you to finance your travels interest-free for a set period, typically 12–21 months. This means you can book flights, accommodations, and experiences now and pay them off over time without accruing interest.

Maximizing Rewards Without the Stress

Many travel rewards cards offer lucrative sign-up bonuses, airline miles, or hotel points. Pairing these benefits with a 0% APR period means you can enjoy free flights, upgrades, or even luxury stays while spreading out payments. It’s a win-win for budget-conscious travelers.

Top 0% Interest Travel Rewards Cards in 2024

1. Chase Sapphire Preferred® Card

0% APR Offer: No introductory APR, but exceptional travel rewards.
Why It Stands Out:
- Earn 60,000 bonus points after spending $4,000 in the first 3 months.
- Points are worth 25% more when redeemed for travel through Chase Ultimate Rewards®.
- No foreign transaction fees.

While this card doesn’t offer a 0% APR, its rewards structure is unmatched. Pair it with a 0% balance transfer card to maximize savings.

2. Bank of America® Travel Rewards Credit Card

0% APR Offer: 0% intro APR for 18 billing cycles on purchases (then 18.24%–28.24% variable).
Why It Stands Out:
- Earn unlimited 1.5x points on all purchases.
- No annual fee and no foreign transaction fees.
- Flexible redemption options for flights, hotels, and more.

This card is perfect for travelers who want simplicity and a lengthy 0% APR period.

3. Wells Fargo Autograph℠ Card

0% APR Offer: 0% intro APR for 12 months on purchases and balance transfers (then 20.24%, 25.24%, or 29.99% variable).
Why It Stands Out:
- Earn 3x points on travel, dining, and gas stations.
- No annual fee and cell phone protection.
- $300 cash bonus after spending $1,500 in the first 3 months.

Ideal for road trippers and frequent flyers alike.

4. Capital One VentureOne Rewards Credit Card

0% APR Offer: 0% intro APR for 15 months on purchases and balance transfers (then 19.99%–29.99% variable).
Why It Stands Out:
- Earn 1.25x miles on every purchase.
- No annual fee and no foreign transaction fees.
- Transfer miles to over 15 airline and hotel partners.

A great option for international travelers seeking flexibility.

How to Make the Most of Your 0% APR Travel Card

Plan Your Spending Wisely

Since the 0% APR period is temporary, create a budget to pay off your balance before the regular APR kicks in. Use tools like spreadsheets or budgeting apps to track expenses.

Leverage Sign-Up Bonuses

Many cards offer hefty bonuses if you meet a minimum spending requirement within the first few months. Time large purchases (e.g., flights, hotels) to qualify for these bonuses.

Avoid Common Pitfalls

  • Late Payments: Missing a payment can void your 0% APR offer. Set up autopay to stay on track.
  • Overspending: Just because you have a 0% APR doesn’t mean you should max out your card. Stick to your travel budget.
  • Foreign Fees: If traveling abroad, choose a card with no foreign transaction fees to save on currency exchange costs.

The Future of Travel Rewards in a Changing Economy

As airlines and hotels adapt to post-pandemic demand, travel rewards programs are becoming more competitive. Some trends to watch:

Dynamic Pricing for Rewards

Airlines are increasingly using dynamic pricing for award flights, meaning the number of points needed can fluctuate. Booking early or during off-peak seasons can save you points.

Sustainability Perks

Eco-conscious travelers can now earn bonus points for choosing green hotels or carbon-neutral flights. Cards like the Amex Green Card reward sustainable spending.

Digital Nomad-Friendly Benefits

With remote work on the rise, some cards offer perks like airport lounge access, travel insurance, and even coworking space credits.

Final Tips for Choosing the Right Card

  • Check Your Credit Score: Most 0% APR travel cards require good to excellent credit (670+ FICO).
  • Compare Fees: Look for cards with no annual fees or fees that are offset by rewards.
  • Read the Fine Print: Understand the APR after the introductory period and any hidden charges.

By selecting the right 0% interest travel rewards card, you can turn your wanderlust into reality without breaking the bank. Happy travels!
