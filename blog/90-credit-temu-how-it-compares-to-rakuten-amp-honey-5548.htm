90 Credit Temu: How It Compares to Rakuten & Honey

Home / Blog / Blog Details

The world of online shopping rewards is more competitive than ever, with platforms like Rakuten and Honey dominating the cashback and coupon space. But now, a new player has entered the scene—90 Credit Temu. This emerging platform promises big savings, but how does it really stack up against the giants? Let’s dive deep into the features, benefits, and drawbacks of each to see which one deserves a spot in your shopping routine.

The Rise of Cashback & Coupon Apps

Online shopping has exploded in recent years, especially with the global shift toward e-commerce accelerated by the pandemic. Consumers are looking for ways to stretch their dollars further, and cashback apps have become a go-to solution.

Why Cashback Matters in Today’s Economy

With inflation hitting record highs and household budgets tightening, every penny saved counts. Cashback apps offer a way to recoup some of your spending without drastically changing shopping habits. Whether it’s Rakuten’s straightforward cashback, Honey’s automatic coupon application, or 90 Credit Temu’s unique rewards system, these platforms are changing how people shop online.

Breaking Down 90 Credit Temu

What Is 90 Credit Temu?

90 Credit Temu is a newer entrant in the rewards space, offering a mix of cashback, discounts, and exclusive deals. Unlike Rakuten and Honey, which have been around for years, Temu is still proving itself—but it brings fresh features that could make it a serious competitor.

Key Features

  • Higher Cashback Rates in Select Categories – Temu sometimes offers higher percentages than Rakuten on certain purchases.
  • Exclusive Member Discounts – Frequent shoppers can unlock additional savings.
  • Referral Bonuses – Strong incentives for bringing in new users.

Potential Drawbacks

  • Limited Merchant Network – Not as many stores as Rakuten or Honey.
  • Payout Delays – Some users report slower cashback processing.

Rakuten: The Cashback Veteran

How Rakuten Works

Rakuten (formerly Ebates) is one of the oldest and most trusted cashback platforms. Users simply shop through Rakuten’s portal or browser extension to earn a percentage back on purchases.

Strengths

  • Wide Retailer Selection – Partnerships with thousands of stores.
  • Quarterly Payouts – Reliable and consistent.
  • Bonus Offers – Frequent promotions for extra cashback.

Weaknesses

  • Lower Rates on Some Stores – Not always the highest cashback available.
  • Browser Extension Can Be Glitchy – Some users report occasional issues.

Honey: The Coupon King

Honey’s Approach

Honey (owned by PayPal) focuses on automatic coupon application at checkout. It also has a cashback program called Honey Gold, but its main appeal is finding the best promo codes.

Why Shoppers Love Honey

  • Effortless Savings – Automatically applies the best discount codes.
  • Honey Gold Rewards – Redeemable for gift cards.
  • Price Tracking – Alerts users when prices drop.

Where It Falls Short

  • Cashback Isn’t as Strong as Rakuten – More focused on coupons.
  • Limited to Online Shopping – No in-store cashback options.

The Verdict: Which One Should You Use?

Best for Maximum Cashback: Rakuten

If your priority is getting the highest percentage back on purchases, Rakuten is still the leader. Its extensive network and reliability make it a top choice.

Best for Automatic Discounts: Honey

For shoppers who hate manually searching for promo codes, Honey is unbeatable. Its seamless integration saves time and money.

Best for Emerging Deals: 90 Credit Temu

If you’re willing to explore a newer platform with potential for higher rewards in certain categories, Temu is worth testing—especially if you shop at its partnered stores.

The Future of Shopping Rewards

As competition heats up, we can expect even more innovation in this space. Will 90 Credit Temu rise to challenge Rakuten and Honey? Only time will tell, but for now, savvy shoppers can benefit from using a combination of these tools to maximize savings.

The key takeaway? Don’t limit yourself to just one—smart shoppers leverage multiple platforms to get the best deals possible.
