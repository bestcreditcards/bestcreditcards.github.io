Universal Credit £1739 Bonus: How to Avoid Missing Out

Home / Blog / Blog Details

The UK’s Universal Credit system has been a lifeline for millions, especially amid rising living costs and economic uncertainty. Recently, news of a potential £1739 bonus has sparked excitement—but also confusion. Many recipients risk missing out simply because they don’t know how to claim it or whether they qualify.

This guide breaks down everything you need to know about the Universal Credit £1739 bonus, eligibility criteria, and actionable steps to ensure you don’t leave money on the table.

What Is the Universal Credit £1739 Bonus?

The £1739 bonus isn’t an automatic payout—it’s a combination of overlooked entitlements, one-time grants, and backdated payments that many claimants fail to claim. Here’s what it typically includes:

1. Cost of Living Payments

The UK government has rolled out several Cost of Living Payments to help low-income households. These are often issued in lump sums (e.g., £299, £300, or £150) and can add up quickly. Missing the deadline or not updating your details could mean losing out.

2. Backdated Claims

If you were eligible for Universal Credit in the past but didn’t apply, you might qualify for backdated payments. The system allows claims up to one month retroactively, which could mean hundreds of pounds extra.

3. Discretionary Housing Payments (DHP)

Struggling with rent? Local councils offer DHPs to cover shortfalls. Many don’t realize they qualify, leaving this extra support unclaimed.

4. Energy Bill Support

With energy prices soaring, additional grants like the Warm Home Discount or Winter Fuel Payment can significantly reduce bills. These are often bundled into the "bonus" figure.

Who Qualifies for the £1739 Bonus?

Not everyone on Universal Credit will get the full £1739, but you might be eligible for parts of it. Key qualifying factors include:

Income Thresholds

  • Your household earnings must fall below a certain level (usually £40,000 or less for most benefits).
  • Savings over £6,000 may reduce your entitlement.

Specific Circumstances

  • Parents: Child Tax Credits or free school meals can add to your total.
  • Disabled claimants: PIP (Personal Independence Payment) or LCWRA (Limited Capability for Work and Related Activity) entitlements boost payouts.
  • Renters: Housing allowances vary by location but are often underclaimed.

How to Claim Your Bonus Before It’s Too Late

Step 1: Check Your Universal Credit Journal

Log into your online account and review your payment history. Look for:
- Missed Cost of Living Payments
- Unclaimed backdated amounts
- Notifications about additional support

Step 2: Update Your Information

Changes in employment, housing, or family size can affect your entitlement. Report these immediately to avoid underpayment.

Step 3: Apply for Top-Up Grants

Many funds (like DHPs or local welfare assistance) require separate applications. Contact your local council or check GOV.UK for open schemes.

Step 4: Seek Free Advice

Organizations like Citizens Advice or Turn2Us offer free benefit checks to ensure you’re claiming everything you’re owed.

Common Mistakes That Make People Miss Out

Assuming Payments Are Automatic

Some bonuses, like DHPs, require active applications. Don’t wait—check and apply.

Ignoring Deadlines

Cost of Living Payments often have cutoff dates. Mark your calendar and set reminders.

Not Appealing Rejections

If your claim is denied, you can appeal. Many successful claimants get approved after challenging decisions.

The Bigger Picture: Why This Matters Now

With inflation still biting and wages struggling to keep up, every pound counts. The £1739 bonus isn’t just extra cash—it’s a buffer against food insecurity, energy poverty, and homelessness.

Governments worldwide are tightening welfare systems, making it harder to access support. In the UK, reforms like the Managed Migration rollout mean some legacy benefits are disappearing. Claiming what you’re owed now ensures stability in uncertain times.

Final Tips to Maximize Your Universal Credit

  • Set up alerts for new government schemes.
  • Keep records of all communications with the DWP.
  • Share this info—many eligible people still don’t know these bonuses exist.

The £1739 bonus could be the difference between struggling and staying afloat. Don’t let bureaucracy or lack of awareness cost you money you deserve.
